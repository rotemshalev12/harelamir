# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Primarily engaged couples planning their wedding, in the emotionally charged window between getting engaged and the event itself. They're likely juggling vendors, budget anxiety, and family expectations, and are looking for someone who will actually be present and accountable rather than a faceless agency. The contact form's event-type field (bar/bat mitzvah, corporate, private party) shows the business also serves other event types, but wedding couples are the page's primary rhetorical audience — the testimonials, hero image, and about copy all speak to them first.

## Product Purpose

A one-page site for הראל אמיר (Harel Amir), an event producer and manager, that sells close, personal, full-service event production — from the first idea through the final moment of the event. Success is a visitor reaching out, ideally via WhatsApp (the path of least friction) or the contact form as a considered fallback.

## Positioning

Not a big, anonymous event-management team — a direct, personal relationship with one producer who is fully present and accountable from planning through the day itself, backed by real industry connections that translate into better pricing and smoother execution.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: Send a WhatsApp message (surfaced in the header, hero, floating button, and footer).
- Secondary CTA: The contact form, for visitors who want to leave structured details (name, phone, event type, date) before a first conversation.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: close, personal accompaniment from the first idea to the last moment of the event — you get to just be present in your own day.
- Belief ladder: (1) this is a real, personal relationship, not an anonymous team; (2) real industry connections and experience mean smarter budget decisions and better prices; (3) on the day itself, full presence and attention to every detail means the couple can actually relax and be in the moment; (4) real couples have had exactly this experience → reach out.
- Proof on hand: three named testimonials (דנה ושחר, נאור ואיתי, צליל ודניאל) with dates, plus a real-event photo gallery linking to Instagram. No additional press, awards, or vendor logos on hand currently.

## Brand Personality

Warm, personal, luxurious. The voice is present-tense and reassuring — it speaks directly to the couple's anxiety about being "just another client" and repeatedly promises closeness, attentiveness, and full presence on the day itself. Confident without being showy; warmth expressed through richness (dark, warm-gold palette) rather than softness or cuteness.

## Anti-references

- Generic templated wedding sites — stock-photo heroes, cookie-cutter Wix/Squarespace wedding layouts.
- Cold corporate/agency sites — anything that reads as a B2B service rather than a personal relationship with one producer.
- The overly pastel, blush, cursive-script "wedding-industry" cliché — the brand's warmth comes from richness and depth, not softness.

## Design Principles

1. **Presence over polish-for-its-own-sake** — every design decision should reinforce personal closeness and accountability, not corporate distance.
2. **Show real people, real moments** — lean on the actual event photos and named testimonials rather than stock imagery, generic iconography, or invented social proof.
3. **Warmth through richness, not pastel** — the dark, warm-gold luxury palette carries the emotional warmth; resist drifting toward blush/pastel wedding-industry defaults.
4. **One clear path to contact, never two competing ones** — WhatsApp stays the frictionless primary path; the form is the considered fallback. Don't let new CTAs dilute this.
5. **Quiet confidence** — elegant restraint over loud gimmicks, so the couple feels safe handing over control of one of the most important days of their life.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA baseline: sufficient color contrast (the dark background against warm gold/cream text needs regular verification), full keyboard navigation, descriptive alt text on all imagery, readable body type sizes, and respect for `prefers-reduced-motion` (already honored in the existing carousel and reveal animations). Hebrew RTL is the sole language; no i18n/LTR variant is in scope.
